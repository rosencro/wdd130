Hiya hey, welcome to the gallery!

This is a collection of various art from recent years, ranging from 2019 to the present!

It's set up into different sections to help it be less overwhelming, and make perusing a lot easier.

There's a wide variety of style conventionally 'good' art here. As a continually growing artist, I decided to include some of the work I'm less proud of. It shows how much I've grown and helps normalize that as an artist, you don't need to produce a masterpiece every time. Being an artist is about creating, whether or not your creation is the next “Starry Night”. And remember that you're still an artist, even if your most recent works are simply doodles in the margins of a notebook.

Anyway, please enjoy this virtual gallery of my art!

Traditional Art

Traditional Art is what most people think of when you talk about art. Traditional Art typically includes works done in pencil, pen, charcoal, pastels, paints, inks, clays, etc. These are works that are done physically, without the aid of a digital program.

Digital Art

Digital Artwork is a form of non-traditional art where the artist creates their work using some kind of program that mimics the medium they wish to work in. Programs like this include Adobe Photoshop/Adobe Illustrator, Clip Studio Paint, Krita, or even MS Paint. Digital art typically offers a kind of flexibility that traditional art cannot by way of being able to edit layers beneath the top layer, the magic of the undo button, duplicating/warping selected areas, etc.

Contrary to popular misconception, the software most digital artists use DOES NOT create the art for them. Software that creates art for you is almost always some kind of AI software (programs/software like Midjourney or Dall-E), which artists do not widely consider to be real art.
